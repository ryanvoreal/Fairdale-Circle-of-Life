About Us

We believe real change starts with community. Our mission is to inspire students and neighbors to work together, share knowledge, and build a better future. From hands-on learning to local initiatives, we’re dedicated to growth, connection, and making a positive impact that lasts.

Change begins with us — learning, growing, and giving back together.

About Fairdale Circle of Care

We are a student-led organization dedicated to supporting our local homeless community through compassion, action, and collaboration. Founded by passionate students at Fairdale High School, we believe in making a tangible difference in our neighborhood.

Our Vision

To create a community where everyone has access to basic needs, support, and hope for a better future.

Our Goals

  • Provide immediate assistance to those in need
  • Educate and raise awareness about homelessness
  • Foster long-term community partnerships
  • Empower students to become community leaders

Our Founders

Fairdale Circle of Care was founded by a group of dedicated students who recognized the need for change in our community. Meet the visionary leaders who started this movement:

Kyleigh Salmon

Co-Founder & Project Coordinator

I’m a junior at Fairdale High School with a passion for social justice and community service. I helped start Fairdale Circle of Care after seeing how many families in my community struggled with food, especially during the holidays. Turning a simple food drive into a student-led competition showed me how powerful compassion and teamwork can be.

"Small actions can make a big difference when people come together."

Contact us

Susan Calderon Hernandez

Co-Founder & Communications Lead

I’m a junior at Fairdale High School, and growing up in an immigrant household shaped my passion for supporting families in need. Through Fairdale Circle of Care, I helped spread awareness across our school and organize a food drive in our first project that brought our community together, collecting over 600 food items in just one week.

"Being able to help others in a way I relate to is what makes this work meaningful."
